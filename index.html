Introduction
What is
CHEMISTRY?
It is one of the major branches of science. It studies the science of matter, the comparison, structure, and properties of substances, as well as the changes that they go through.
×
Chemistry is sometimes called the “central science” because it provides a foundation for understanding both basic and applied scientific disciplines at a fundamental level.

It is also known as experimental science because of the cause-and-effect relationship, wherein it will start out with a hypothesis, then conduct an experiment that will lead to a conclusion. It is performed under controlled conditions in an activity and observes a natural or artificially created phenomenon. Because of chemistry, we can come up with things that haven't been discovered yet.
STATE OF MATTER
SOLID, LIQUID & GAS
According to Anne Helmenstin (2022), matter is anything that has mass and takes up space. It consists of subatomic particles, atoms, ions, and compounds.
×
Sometimes these particles are tightly bound and close together, while other times they are loosely connected and widely separated. States of matter describe the qualities displayed by matter. Basically, the state of matter of a substance depends on how much energy its particles have.
×

S O L I D

According to (Lemon, Jones, 2003), the solid modeling approach completely and unambiguously defines the volume of a three-dimensional object. Solid models can be manipulated via set operations. For example, a new solid can be created by computing the volumetric union, difference, or intersection of two solids. This feature can be a powerful tool when constructing detailed, three-dimensional mechanical parts.

While several methods have been developed for defining solid models, the most popular method is called “boundary representation.” With the boundary representation method, a solid model is defined by representing the outer surface of the solid. This surface is a collection of individual faces, typically quadrilaterals or triangles. Each face defines either a linear surface patch or a higher-order surface such as a Bezier or B-spline surface.
×

L I Q U I D

According to Ingebrigsten, Schroder, and Dyre (2020), simple liquids are traditionally defined as many-body systems of classical particles interacting via radially symmetric pair potentials. According to Ryu and Egami (2020), liquid fragility characterizes how steeply the viscosity of a glass-forming liquid decreases with increasing temperature above the glass transition. It is one of the most fundamental properties of a liquid, with high importance for science and application.
×

G A S

According to Zavala-Araiza et al. (2015) natural gas has been described as a central element of the clean energy transition. According to Lissianski, Zamansky, and Gardiner Jr. (2012) a chemical reaction is the conversion of one kind of matter into another chemically different form; a combustion reaction is a fast chemical reaction in which a fuel combines with an oxidizer (usually oxygen from air) to form combustion products. Combustion reactions are described by writing the chemical symbols for the starting materials, the reactants, and the final products separated by an arrow that means "react with one another to form.".

FREEZING POINT

It is the temperature at which a liquid becomes a solid. As with the melting point, increased pressure usually raises the freezing point.

MATERIALS: Water , Containers And Refrigerator

PROCEDURE: First, find any container that is placed by the water; second, put the water inside the container; and third, after putting the water in the container, put it into the refrigerator until it becomes solid material.
×

MELTING POINT

The melting point is the temperature at which the solid and liquid forms of a pure substance can exist in equilibrium. As heat is applied to a solid, its temperature will increase until the melting point is reached. More heat will then convert the solid into a liquid. 

MATERIALS: Ice

PROCEDURE: As the ice is heated, the motion of the molecules increases.

SUBLIMATION

The gaseous phase without changing into the liquid phase. This process is an endothermic phase transition that occurs at a temperature and pressure below the triple point of the substance.  

MATERIALS: Airspray

PROCEDURE: First, spray the air inside the restroom. After that, close the door immediately. Lastly, Observe the sublimation inside the restroom.

MAIN PROPERTIES
OF GAS
It includes their high compressibility, allowing them to be easily squeezed into smaller volumes under pressure. It also exhibits low densities compared to liquids and solids, resulting in their ability to diffuse rapidly and fill the entirety of their container.

VAPOR PRESSURE

Vapor pressure is a measure of the tendency of a material to change into the gaseous or vapor state, and it increases with temperature. 

MATERIALS: Water, Ice , Can, & Pan

PROCEDURE: First, put the pan on fire. Second, put water inside the pan, and lastly, observe the water to see if boiling happens and some vaporization occurs.
×

BOILING POINT

Boiling point: the temperature at which the pressure exerted by the surroundings upon a liquid is equaled by the pressure exerted by the vapor of the liquid.

MATERIALS: Any pan , Water and Fire only

PROCEDURE: First, put the water on any pan; second, boil the water until bubbles form; and well done, that's the boiling point.
